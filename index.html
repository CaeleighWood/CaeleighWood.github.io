Get to know me

My name is Caeleigh Joy Wood

My Travels

I was born in Iron Mountain, MI and lived in Dunbar WI for 6.5 years. My father felt called to go minister in the islands, so we moved to Guam. I lived there for another 5 years until we decided to move back. We moved back to Taylors, SC and I have lived here ever since.

My Present Life

I am currently a Sophomore at Bob Jones University and a cross town student at Clemson University. I accepted the challenge of being a cross town cadet of Clemson's AFROTC, Detachment 770. I'm also enrolled in flight school at USAEROFlight and have almost reached my next goal, a pilots license.

My Future Plans

Upon graduation, I am on track to commission into the Air Force as a 2nd Lieutenant. My overall dream would be to become a fighter pilot flying F-22's, but this is a challenging and competitive role, so my other desired roles are AMB, CSO, and CRO.

About

Here's a little bit about my story!

  • 2004-20011

    My Beginning


    I, along with my twin brother, was born in Iron Mountain, MI. I am considered a Uper even though I was not raised in the Upper Peninsula. My family, consisting of 2 loving parents and an awesome older brother, lived in Dunbar, WI at the time. For 6.5 years, I learned the ways of the northern people. 

  • 2011-2015

    The Great Migration

    After being a part of the ministry in the North, My father felt called to go minister in the islands, so we moved to Guam, a territory of the USA in the middle of the Pacific Ocean. I lived there for another 5 years, learning all I could about the islander way of life until we decided to move back in 2015 for many different reasons.


  • 2015-2023

    Aaaand I'm back.


    After our years in the Islands and the ministry we completed there,We moved back to Taylors, SC. My father's mother lives here and my father grew up here. For a few years, my father was the Vice President of Enrollment and Marketing at Bob Jones University!

  • 2004-2023

    Mega Recap

    While I still have no clue how to answer the question, "Where are you from," I am grateful for my culture induced past as it has taught me to enjoy and be thankful for the little things of life and the people I meet along the way. 

  • Questions? Reach out to me!
